Pemayangtse Monastery

Pemayangtse Monastery, located near Pelling in West Sikkim, is one of the most significant and historically rich monasteries in the region. As a premier institution of the Nyingma sect of Tibetan Buddhism, it holds a unique place in Sikkim's cultural and spiritual heritage.

History & Significance

The monastery was founded in 1705 by Lama Lhatsun Chempo, one of the three founding lamas who played a crucial role in the consecration of the first Chogyal (king) of Sikkim at Yuksom. This historical connection firmly establishes its importance in the history of the Sikkimese kingdom.

Pemayangtse was built for "ta-tshang" or "pure monks," who were required to be celibate and of pure Tibetan lineage. This practice, though from an earlier time, is still retained, and only the monks of this monastery are entitled to this prestigious title.

The head lama of Pemayangtse Monastery had the distinct privilege of anointing the Chogyals of Sikkim with holy water, symbolizing the close relationship between the spiritual and royal powers of the kingdom.

Festivals, Events, and Rituals

The monastery is a living center of Buddhist practice and is famous for its elaborate festivals.

Cham Dance Festival

The most important event at the monastery is the annual Cham dance festival, held on the 28th and 29th day of the 12th Tibetan lunar month (usually in February). During this two-day festival, monks perform sacred masked dances to ward off evil and bless the coming year. The event culminates with a display of a giant embroidered thangka (religious scroll) and a firework display, which symbolizes the driving away of evil spirits.

Pang Lhabsol

This unique Sikkimese festival, which honors Mount Khangchendzonga, the guardian deity of Sikkim, is also celebrated at the monastery. The festival features a special warrior dance performed by the monks known as Pangtoed Chaam.

Plan Your Visit

Visiting Hours & Entry

  • Timings: Generally open from 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM.
  • Entry Fee: Nominal fee of around INR 20 per person.

How to Get There

  • Location: Near Pelling, West Sikkim.
  • By Road: Easy access by taxi or foot from Pelling.
  • By Air: Nearest airport is Bagdogra (IXB).
  • By Rail: Nearest station is New Jalpaiguri (NJP).

Best Time to Visit

  • Spring (March-May): Pleasant weather and clear views.
  • Autumn (September-November): Crisp skies and panoramic views.
  • Winter (Dec-Feb): Cold, but ideal for the annual Cham dance festival.

Tips for Visitors

  • Dress Code: Wear clothing that covers shoulders and knees.
  • Footwear: Remove shoes before entering the prayer hall.
  • Silence: Maintain a quiet and respectful atmosphere.
  • Photography: Ask for permission before taking pictures, as it may be restricted in certain areas.
